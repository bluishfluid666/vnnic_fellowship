Vietnam's Internet Infrastructure as a Catalyst for Innovation

Exploring how Vietnam's digital backbone—from the .vn domain to VNIX—can drive AI, IoT, and technological breakthroughs in the digital age

Introduction

As a committed citizen of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam, with deep interest in internet governance and a background in Computer Science, I find Vietnam's rapid digital transformation truly inspiring. The country's commitment to achieving major breakthroughs in science, technology, and innovation—as highlighted in the Politburo's Resolution 57-NQ/TW—demonstrates not only ambition, but also a clear understanding of how crucial a robust internet infrastructure is for national development.

Key resources such as the country-code domain .vn, IP addresses and Autonomous System Numbers (ASNs), the DNS system, and especially the Vietnam National Internet Exchange (VNIX) form the essential pillars of Vietnam's internet landscape. From my perspective, leveraging and further innovating on these foundational elements is the way forward for Vietnam to unlock its creative potential and accelerate the growth of its digital economy.

The ".vn" Domain: Fostering Digital Identity and Sovereignty

The .vn country-code top-level domain is more than just an address on the Internet; it is a cornerstone of Vietnam's digital identity and sovereignty. Since Vietnam first connected the ".vn" domain to the global Internet in 1994, it has grown into a trusted, secure, and recognized digital identity for businesses, individuals, and government agencies.

As of 2024, over 630,000 .vn domains are registered, ranking Vietnam second in ASEAN and 10th in Asia-Pacific. This broad adoption reflects how domain names underpin nearly all online activities and services. The government recognizes that a strong national domain space can accelerate digital transformation and the digital economy.

A national program (2024–2025) is underway to expand .vn registrations to 1 million, with initiatives like new domain categories (id.vn, biz.vn, io.vn, ai.vn) and fee incentives for youth and small businesses.

These efforts underscore how the .vn domain facilitates innovation by lowering barriers for entrepreneurs to establish an online presence, encouraging local content creation, and reinforcing trust in Vietnamese online services. Using the national domain also contributes to protecting digital sovereignty—ensuring Vietnam's online presence is under domestic jurisdiction and control.

IP Addresses and ASN: Building a Future-Ready Network

Behind every Internet connection are IP addresses and Autonomous System Numbers (ASNs)—critical resources that VNNIC (Vietnam Internet Network Information Center) allocates to ISPs, enterprises, and other network operators. Over the past decades, Vietnam's Internet has expanded from a single network in 1997 to 1,148 independent networks today, all interconnected via IP and ASN systems.

To drive innovation further, Vietnam has been a leader in adopting IPv6, the next-generation IP addressing protocol. As of late 2024, IPv6 adoption in Vietnam reached 65.5%, ranking 7th worldwide (and 2nd in ASEAN)—far above the global average.

IPv6: The Foundation for IoT and Innovation

This forward-looking transition is crucial: IPv6 provides an abundant address space to connect the explosion of IoT sensors, devices, and services that an innovation-driven economy needs. Vietnam's push toward an IPv6-only Internet by 2030 (with targets of 70% IPv6 usage by 2025) is directly aligned with the demands of IoT, 5G, and cloud computing.

Over 800 Vietnamese network operators have implemented RPKI digital resource signing to prevent BGP routing hijacks and misconfigurations, resulting in a 73% reduction in routing security incidents in 2024.

Strengthening DNS Infrastructure for Resilience and Trust

The Domain Name System (DNS) is often called the "phone book" of the Internet—it translates human-friendly names (like example.vn) to numeric IP addresses. A robust DNS infrastructure is vital for both day-to-day operations and long-term digital sovereignty.

Vietnam has invested in domestic DNS root servers and caching systems, which has significantly improved domain resolution speeds (by up to five times) for Vietnamese users. By hosting root DNS mirrors and critical DNS servers inside the country, Vietnam not only accelerates access to all internet services but also ensures that DNS queries can be resolved even if international connectivity is disrupted.

Security and Innovation

Vietnam's DNS administration supports DNSSEC (DNS Security Extensions), which adds a layer of authenticity to DNS answers and helps prevent cyberattacks like domain spoofing. As Vietnam's digital economy grows, protecting domain queries and responses from tampering is essential—especially for e-government, fintech, and health platforms where trust is paramount.

VNIX: From Traffic Exchange Point to Digital Backbone

The Vietnam National Internet Exchange (VNIX) stands at the heart of Vietnam's Internet transformation—evolving from a simple traffic exchange point into the potential "digital backbone" for the nation's AI and IoT future.

Current State of VNIX

Established as a non-profit, neutral peering point, VNIX has been instrumental in connecting ISPs and improving Internet quality in Vietnam. By allowing local networks to exchange traffic directly, VNIX keeps domestic internet traffic within the country, which improves speeds and reduces dependence on costly international links.

Today VNIX is recognized as one of the key infrastructures of Vietnam's Internet, crucial for safety and development. It has evolved over 18+ years from just two founding members (VNPT and Viettel) to over 30 networks, including not only ISPs but also content providers, data centers, cloud services and government networks.

The Vision: VNIX as a Digital Backbone for AI and IoT

However, to truly drive the "breakthrough" innovations envisioned by Resolution 57, VNIX can evolve beyond a basic exchange. The emerging vision is for VNIX to serve as a "Digital Backbone" for AI and IoT systems across Vietnam—not merely a conduit for traffic, but an active platform supporting the next generation of applications.

Rather than only switching packets between networks, VNIX could host distributed computing resources at its exchange nodes, creating an edge computing ecosystem within the country.

Edge Computing at VNIX Nodes

Under this model, VNIX would deploy clusters of edge servers at strategic exchange points (for example, in Hanoi, Da Nang, Ho Chi Minh City, and additional regional hubs). These mini data centers, directly connected to VNIX's high-speed switching fabric, would store and process data closer to end users and devices. The goal is to support domestic AI and IoT applications—especially in critical sectors like agriculture, healthcare, and education—with minimal latency and maximum data sovereignty.

For instance, an IoT network of smart farms in the Mekong Delta can send sensor readings to a VNIX edge cluster in Ho Chi Minh City for immediate analysis and feedback on irrigation or pest control, rather than having to send data to a distant cloud server overseas. Similarly, a hospital in central Vietnam using an AI system to assist in medical imaging diagnostics could quickly access computing power at the nearest VNIX node to analyze results, enabling faster clinical decisions while keeping sensitive health data within Vietnam's borders.

Learning from International Models

Vietnam's vision for VNIX as a digital backbone aligns with global trends where Internet infrastructure is evolving to meet the demands of AI and IoT. Around the world, we see a convergence of Internet Exchange Points (IXPs) and edge computing to support latency-sensitive applications.

Recent studies of U.S. exchanges highlight the need for connectivity to move "closer to the edge" as latency-sensitive applications boom, noting that distributed, neutral IXPs enable faster data processing nearer to end-users—critical for IoT, AI, and real-time analytics. In Europe, industry experts argue that the traditional model of a few centralized exchanges is giving way to more regional IXPs paired with edge data centers.

By deploying exchanges and data centers in regional areas, countries are reducing bottlenecks and latency while enhancing resilience and supporting growth in local tech hubs.

These international trends validate Vietnam's direction: turning VNIX into a distributed backbone with edge nodes is an "ahead-of-the-curve" strategy. Few ASEAN countries have yet developed strong regional exchange + edge networks; Vietnam could lead by example, much as it did with IPv6 adoption in ASEAN.

Innovative Ideas and Recommendations

Expand VNIX into a Nationwide Edge Cloud
Deploy small-scale data centers at VNIX exchange points across Vietnam, hosting edge computing clusters open for use by Vietnamese startups, AI researchers, and IoT platforms. Offer computing power on a neutral platform that can incubate new services requiring low latency.
Sector-Focused Edge Initiatives
Prioritize edge deployments for Vietnam's socio-economic priorities: agriculture, healthcare, and education. Create pilot programs like "Smart Agriculture Edge" and "Healthcare Edge Clusters" to demonstrate value and attract industry buy-in.
Leverage .vn and DNS for Local Content Delivery
Encourage .vn domains for all Vietnam-focused IoT and AI services, integrating DNS to resolve domestic queries to local VNIX-connected servers when available, ensuring local traffic stays local.
Promote IPv6 and ASN for Innovation Networks
Create an "IoT addressing incubator" where VNNIC allocates dedicated IPv6 prefixes to innovative IoT projects and simplify the process for startups to obtain ASNs and connect to VNIX.
Strengthen Collaboration and Skills
Establish a VNNIC Academy focusing on emerging network technologies and involve local universities in testbeds hosted on VNIX's infrastructure to spark creative uses and ensure skilled professionals.
Policy and Incentives
Offer tax incentives for companies utilizing .vn domains, IPv6, or VNIX edge services. Update data governance frameworks to encourage data sharing via VNIX's backbone while protecting privacy.

Conclusion

Reflecting on Vietnam's path forward, I truly believe that our ability to achieve innovation, creativity, and meaningful breakthroughs in the digital age will rest on how wisely we make use of our national Internet resources. The .vn domain offers a uniquely Vietnamese digital identity for anyone hoping to create, build, or connect online. The nation's push for IPv6 and strengthening of IP networks lays essential groundwork for universal, high-quality connectivity, while resilient DNS infrastructure guarantees that Vietnamese services remain fast and reliable.

But at the heart of this transformation is VNIX—the Vietnam National Internet Exchange. It has the potential to move far beyond its original purpose, evolving into the true "digital backbone" for Vietnam's AI and IoT ambitions. If we can embed edge computing at the core of VNIX and ensure that data is processed locally, we can sharply reduce latency, boost service quality, and keep sensitive information within our own borders.

What makes Vietnam stand out is our strong political commitment—as shown by Resolution 57—and the rare chance to build modern infrastructure for the 21st century almost from scratch.

Ultimately, if we continue to innovate in how we manage and leverage our Internet domains, addressing, DNS, and exchange points, I'm convinced we can set off a virtuous cycle: empowering local tech communities with the speed, reliability, and trust they need to create world-class digital products, which will in turn propel Vietnam's broader digital transformation. By making VNIX the "digital backbone" of our AI and IoT future, we can lay the foundation for an era of innovation that is faster, safer, and unmistakably Vietnamese—helping our country stand tall as a leader in technology and creativity in the years ahead.
